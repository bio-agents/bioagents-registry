What is bio.agents?

The use of bioinformatics is ubiquitous within the life sciences. In bio.agents, we are striving to provide a comprehensive registry of software and databases, facilitating researchers from across the spectrum of b iological and biomedical science to find, understand, utilise and cite the resources they need in their day-to-day work.

Everything from simple command-line agents and online services, through to databases and complex, multi-functional analysis workflows is included. Resources are described in a rigorous semantics and syntax, providing end-users with the convenience of concise, consistent and therefore comparable information.

Each bio.agents entry is assigned a human-readable, unique identifier based on the resource name, e.g. bioagents:signalp. These identifiers provide a persistent reference to our "Agent Cards" of essential information, as well as a means to trace resources and integrate bio.agents data with other resources.

All the bio.agents data and technical components are available under open license and we warmly welcome you to get involved. bio.agents development is supported by IECHOR - the European infrastructure for life science information.

bio.agents was supported by IECHOR through the IECHOR-EXCELERATE grant, which was funded by the European Union Horizon 2020 program under grant agreement 676559.

Agent IDs

All bio.agents entries are assigned a human-friendly unique identifier, e.g. bioagents:signalp. Once verified, a bio.agents ID provides a stable way to trace resources and integrate bio.agents data with other projects.

Standard Semantics

The scientific function of bio.agents resources can be precisely annotated in defined terms from the EDAM ontology, including common topics, operations, types of data and data formats.

Standard Syntax

bio.agents resource descriptions adhere to a rigorous syntax defined by bioagentsSchema, which provides regular expressions, controlled vocabularies and other syntax rules for 50 key attributes.
