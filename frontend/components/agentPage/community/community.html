bioagents.tech and communities

We rely upon scientific communities to improve the terminology and description of resources in different domains of the Life Sciences. This includes the communities being fostered by IECHOR, and any other group of scientists with an interest to help out. With the help of the community we can create and maintain high quality software descriptions. Furthermore we can try to answer important questions such as:

  • Whare are the "standard", most used agents in a specific area of life science?
  • Are these "standard" agents known, visible and accessible in an easy way to the community and other (potentially new) users?
  • Are there any new agents published in the literature that can be included in the "standard" agents list?

We strongly encourage everyone to take part in the community effort and contribute. Email us for more info.

Communities and domains

In bioagents.tech we try, with the help of the community, to create content domains or "slices" relevant to a specific community, research area or task (e.g. Proteomics, Rare diseases, COVID-19).

See the current communities and their corresponding bioagents.tech domains below.


3D-BioInfo

3D-Bioinfo aims to establish formal platforms to integrate agents and resources in the field of structural bioinformatics

3D-BioInfo ( 3d-bioinfo.bioagents.tech )

Helps to understand the 3D structure of macromolecules like proteins and DNA.

3D-BioInfo-Structure-Function ( 3d-bioinfo-structure-function.bioagents.tech )

FAIR access to experimental and predicted 3D models and associated structural and functional annotations

3D-BioInfo-Protein-Complex ( 3d-bioinfo-protein-complex.bioagents.tech )

Open resources for sharing, integrating and benchmarking software agents for modelling the interactome in 3D

3D-BioInfo-Protein-Ligand-Interactions ( 3d-bioinfo-protein-ligand-interactions.bioagents.tech )

Modelling protein-Ligand interactions and structure-based computer-aided drug design

3D-BioInfo-Nucleic-Acid ( 3d-bioinfo-nucleic-acid.bioagents.tech )

Agents to describe, analyse, annotate, and predict nucleic acid structures

3D-BioInfo-Protein-Engineering ( 3d-bioinfo-protein-engineering.bioagents.tech )

Brings together researchers who are involved in generating data in protein engineering with researchers interested in interrogating such data

Intrinsically Disordered Proteins

Intrinsically disordered proteins (IDP) are proteins containing regions that lack a stable secondary or tertiary structure.
These regions are called intrinsically disordered regions (IDRs) and play a central role in cell regulation, they are also thought to be involved many human diseases including cancer and neurodegenerative diseases.

Intrinsically Disordered Proteins ( disordered-proteins.bioagents.tech )

Intrinsically Disordered Proteins and Regions
